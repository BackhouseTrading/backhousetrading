Privacy Policy

How Backhouse Trading, LLC collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Backhouse Trading, LLC ("Backhouse Trading," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who visit our website or contact us. This Privacy Policy describes what information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, and the choices you have regarding your information.

This policy applies to information collected through our website at backhousetrading.com and through direct communications with our team. It does not apply to information collected through other means, such as through separate client agreements or regulatory filings.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

When you contact us through our website contact form, email, or telephone, we may collect:

  • Your name and title
  • Your firm or organization
  • Your email address and phone number
  • Your investor type and the nature of your inquiry
  • Any other information you choose to include in your message

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • IP address (anonymized where technically feasible)
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Referring URL
  • Date and time of access

This information is collected through server logs and, where applicable, analytics tools. We do not use advertising cookies or cross-site tracking cookies.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiries: We use your contact information to reply to requests for information, the Disclosure Document, or a scheduled conversation.
  • To communicate about our program: If you are a current or prospective client, we may use your information to provide performance reports, program updates, and other communications required by or relevant to your investment relationship.
  • To comply with legal obligations: We may use your information to comply with applicable laws, regulations (including CFTC and NFA requirements), legal process, or governmental requests.
  • To improve our website: Aggregated, anonymized analytics data is used to understand how visitors use our website and to improve its content and functionality.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. We do not use your information for automated decision-making or profiling.

Information Sharing

We may share your information with third parties only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We may share information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our website, processing contact form submissions, or providing other services necessary to our operations. These providers are contractually obligated to use your information only to provide services to us and in accordance with this Privacy Policy.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, including to regulators such as the CFTC or NFA.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of our assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change in ownership or control.
  • With your consent: We may share your information in other circumstances with your express consent.

Financial Privacy Notice (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act)

To the extent Backhouse Trading is considered a "financial institution" under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), we are required to provide you with a notice of our privacy practices with respect to nonpublic personal information (NPI). In accordance with GLBA:

  • We collect NPI from you when you contact us or enter into an advisory relationship with us.
  • We do not disclose NPI about you to nonaffiliated third parties except as described in this Privacy Policy and as permitted by law.
  • We protect NPI through appropriate physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards.
  • We restrict access to NPI to those employees and service providers who need to know that information to serve you.

California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have certain rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including:

  • Right to Know: You may request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the purposes for which we use it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.
  • Right to Delete: You may request that we delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Correct: You may request that we correct inaccurate personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale: We do not sell personal information. If this practice changes, we will provide notice and the right to opt out.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA/CPRA rights.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at compliance@backhousetrading.com. We will respond to verified requests within 45 days.

International Visitors (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you may have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or equivalent legislation, including the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data, the right to restrict or object to processing, and the right to data portability.

Our legal basis for processing your information is generally our legitimate interest in responding to inquiries and operating our business, or your consent where expressly provided. We do not transfer personal data to countries outside the EEA without appropriate safeguards.

To exercise your GDPR rights, please contact us at compliance@backhousetrading.com.

Cookies and Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies for the following limited purposes:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: If we use website analytics tools (such as Google Analytics), these tools may use cookies to collect anonymized information about website usage. IP addresses are anonymized where possible. We do not use analytics data for targeted advertising.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality. We do not use advertising cookies, retargeting cookies, or social media cookies.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, to comply with legal obligations, or to resolve disputes. Contact form submissions are typically retained for a period of up to three years unless there is an ongoing advisory relationship or legal requirement to retain the information longer.

Data Security

We implement reasonable physical, electronic, and administrative safeguards to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Children's Privacy

This website is not directed at children under the age of 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete such information.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. We will post the updated policy on this page with a revised effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Your continued use of this website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
