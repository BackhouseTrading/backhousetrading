Program Description

The Discretionary
Macro Program

The program combines two complementary disciplines: discretionary trade selection—drawing on macro judgment, positioning analysis, and observational price action—with rules-based risk management that enforces disciplined capital allocation on every trade.

The result is a program designed to capitalize on market dislocations while maintaining an asymmetric payoff profile: defined, limited downside risk on each trade with uncapped upside participation.

Discretionary

Trade selection—which markets, which direction, when to enter and exit—is fully discretionary, drawing on the manager's judgment and multi-disciplinary analysis.

Rules-Based

Risk management is entirely rule-based. Position size, stop placement, and portfolio construction follow a strict, non-negotiable framework regardless of conviction level.

Trading Philosophy

Three principles that guide every decision

Adapt to market regime

Markets cycle through distinct regimes—trending, mean-reverting, volatile, quiet; monetary-driven or fiscal-driven. Rather than optimizing a single strategy for one regime, we adapt our approach to what the current market is rewarding.

Observe, don't predict

The future is unknowable. We manage risk carefully, actively seek convexity, and position to participate in structural moves as they unfold—reacting to the present rather than forecasting the future.

Discretion wins at inflection points

Systematic models are trained on historical data and are structurally slow to recognize regime changes. A disciplined discretionary process can identify and act on market inflection points before systematic programs adapt.

Investment Process

Multi-disciplinary
filtering framework

Trade ideas are filtered through three independent analytical disciplines. A trade must pass all three gates before capital is committed. The process is designed to exclude most ideas and maximize the risk/reward of those that remain.

Identify a Macro Bias

Top-Down Macro Trends

Does the long-term macroeconomic backdrop provide a meaningful tailwind to the trade? We assess interest rate cycles, currency regimes, commodity supply/demand dynamics, and geopolitical flows.

Bottom-Up Sector Analysis

Are the trade's fundamental assumptions consistent with what companies and policymakers are reporting? Earnings calls, management guidance, and policy communications are cross-referenced against the trade thesis.

Find a Positioning Advantage

Commitments of Traders (COT) Report

Does the CFTC's weekly positioning data reveal an asymmetric risk/reward setup? Extreme speculative positioning often precedes reversals; light positioning can allow trends to extend further than consensus expects.

Narrative & Sentiment

Does the manager hold a differentiated view relative to the prevailing market consensus? The best opportunities arise when positioning is extreme but the narrative hasn't yet turned.

Wait for a Catalyst

Price Action in Context

Given the macro and positioning context, how is price behaving in response to new information? Price action that confirms the thesis—particularly at key technical levels—provides the catalyst for entry.

Correlation Breakdown

Are there assets moving in unexpected or anomalous ways relative to historical relationships? Correlation breakdowns often signal a regime shift before it becomes consensus, providing early entry opportunities.

Risk Management

A non-negotiable framework for every trade

Trade-Level Rules

Stop loss level established before entry—never adjusted in the direction of risk
Position size determined by: stop distance, entry level, and 1% capital-at-risk limit
Standard trades risk 1% of total capital plus estimated slippage
Trade is closed immediately if the original thesis is invalidated
Always maintain the correct side of convexity

Portfolio-Level Rules

Maximum three concurrent positions at any time
Correlated positions are sized down proportionally to manage aggregate risk
A 3-position portfolio must contain at least one negatively correlated position
Average margin-to-equity ratio maintained near 5%
