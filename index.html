Trading Philosophy

Three principles that guide every trade

Markets are in a constant state of change

We adapt to the market and trade whatever opportunities are available, rather than forcing a single strategy across all regimes. Trend, mean-reversion, monetary or fiscal dominance—each regime is an opportunity.

The future is unknowable—we observe and react

We do not make predictions. We manage risk, seek out convexity, and position to take advantage of structural moves as they develop in real time, not as models dictated they should.

Discretion outperforms at inflection points

Systematic models optimize against historical regimes and underfit to current market reality. A disciplined discretionary trader can recognize and act on structural shifts before systematic programs adapt.

Investment Process

A three-stage
filtering framework

Each trade must clear three independent analytical layers—fundamental, positioning, and observational—before capital is deployed. Most ideas are rejected.

Identify a Macro Bias

Top-Down Macro Trends

Does my long-term macro view provide a meaningful tailwind to the trade thesis?

Bottom-Up Sector Analysis

Are the trade's assumptions consistent with what companies are reporting and what CEOs are communicating?

Find a Positioning Advantage

Commitments of Traders (COT)

Does positioning data suggest an asymmetric risk/reward payoff relative to consensus?

Narrative & Sentiment

Do I hold a differentiated view compared to the prevailing market narrative?

Wait for a Catalyst

Price Action in Context

Given market context, how is price reacting to new information—confirming or contradicting the thesis?

Correlation Breakdown

Are any assets moving in unexpected or historically anomalous ways, signaling a regime shift?

Risk Management Rules

Standard trades risk 1% of capital per position Stop level set prior to entry—never after Maximum 3 concurrent positions Correlated positions sized down proportionally 3-position portfolio must include 1 negative correlated position Exit immediately if original thesis is invalidated Always maintain correct side of convexity Average margin-to-equity ratio: ~5%
