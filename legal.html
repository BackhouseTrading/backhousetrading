Qualified Eligible Persons Only

The investment programs described on this website are available only to Qualified Eligible Persons (QEPs) as defined in CFTC Regulation 4.7, or to persons who are otherwise qualified under applicable law. This website is not directed at retail investors or members of the general public.

A Qualified Eligible Person generally includes: (i) registered commodity pool operators or commodity trading advisors; (ii) banks, insurance companies, registered investment companies, business development companies, and small business investment companies; (iii) certain employee benefit plans; (iv) any natural person with at least $2 million invested in commodity interests or $200,000 net worth and certain other qualifications; and (v) other persons meeting CFTC Regulation 4.7 requirements. Prospective investors should consult their legal counsel to determine whether they qualify as QEPs.

Risk Disclosures

Trading commodity futures and options on futures involves a high degree of risk and is not appropriate for all investors. The following risks, among others, are associated with commodity trading:

  • Loss of capital: You may lose your entire investment and, in some circumstances, more than your initial investment due to the leveraged nature of futures contracts.
  • Leverage risk: Futures contracts involve significant leverage. Small movements in market prices can result in large profits or losses relative to the capital deployed.
  • Liquidity risk: Although the program is constrained to highly liquid markets, market conditions may at times reduce the ability to execute trades at desired prices.
  • Counterparty risk: Trading through a futures commission merchant (FCM) involves counterparty risk. Client assets are held in segregated accounts pursuant to CFTC regulations, but this does not eliminate all risk.
  • Manager risk: The performance of the program depends substantially on the judgment and decisions of a single portfolio manager. The incapacity or departure of the manager could materially affect the program's operations and results.
  • Model and judgment risk: Investment decisions involve judgment and discretion. No assurance can be given that any particular investment will be successful.
  • Regulatory risk: Changes in applicable laws and regulations could adversely affect the program's operations or the treatment of gains and losses for tax purposes.
  • Tax risk: The tax treatment of futures contracts under IRC Section 1256 may change, and the tax consequences of investing may vary depending on individual circumstances. Prospective investors should consult their tax advisors.
